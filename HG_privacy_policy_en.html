HizmetGetir — Privacy Policy & KVKK Disclosure Notice

Effective date: 16 June 2026

This notice explains how your personal data is processed when you use the HizmetGetir mobile application (the “App”) and serves as a disclosure (aydınlatma) notice under Law No. 6698 on the Protection of Personal Data (“KVKK”).

1. Data Controller

Data controller: Muhammed Kasım Adıgüzel (HizmetGetir / APRYCO).
Contact: ahmetadqzl0244@gmail.com

2. Personal Data We Process

All users: phone number; password (kept hashed by Firebase Authentication; we do not store it in plain text).

Pros:

  • The name and surname and area of expertise (category) read by AI from the mastership certificate.
  • An irreversible verification summary derived from the certificate, in order to prevent abuse (e.g. opening multiple accounts with the same certificate). The certificate image and its other personal fields (national ID number, parents' names, birth details, etc.) are not stored (see section 4).
  • HGP (HizmetGetir currency) balance, ratings and reviews.

Listings (entered by the customer): service category, problem description, province and district, listing status; the phone number opened to the other party after matching.

The App does not collect your full address, national ID number, location (GPS) or payment-card data. Matching is only at the province/district level and is not kept as a stored address — it is used only for that match. The exact address is shared between the parties themselves.

Data is collected directly from you, electronically and by fully or partly automated means while you use the App. No special-category personal data (such as health or biometric data) is processed (the photo on the certificate is not used or stored for biometric purposes). The App is intended for users aged 18 and over.

3. Processing Purposes and Legal Grounds

  • Account creation and authentication — establishment/performance of a contract.
  • Verification of the pro's certificate and protection of platform trust — legitimate interest and explicit consent.
  • Matching customer and pro — performance of a contract.
  • Management of HGP balance and transactions — performance of a contract.
  • Fulfilling legal obligations — legal obligation.

4. Mastership Certificate and AI Verification

To keep the platform professional, pros are asked for a mastership certificate. The certificate you upload:

  • Is sent to an AI service for one-time verification only; the service reads the name-surname and area of expertise, and also checks whether the certificate appears genuine.
  • For this, the certificate is processed on the infrastructure of the overseas service provider Google (Gemini API).
  • The certificate image is not stored after analysis. Only the name-surname, category and an irreversible verification summary that prevents abuse are associated with your account.
  • No other information on the certificate is stored — including the certificate image/copy, national ID number, certificate and serial number, date and place of birth, mother's and father's names, gender, and the photo on the certificate.

The processing and overseas transfer of the certificate for one-time verification rely on your explicit consent; you give this consent by ticking the separate checkbox in the App before uploading your certificate. You may withdraw this consent at any time (see section 8).

Certificate verification is performed automatically (by AI). If you disagree with the result, you may request a human review or object via the contact address above.

5. Payments (HGP)

In-app access uses HGP (HizmetGetir currency); HGP is spent by both the customer and the pro when a match occurs (posting a listing is free). HGP is purchased through the App Store / Google Play; your card details are not transmitted to us. The fee for the work itself is outside the platform, between the parties.

6. Data Transfers

  • Google — certificate verification (Gemini) and infrastructure services (Firebase: authentication, database, notifications).
  • Apple App Store / Google Play — HGP purchases.

As some of these providers are located abroad, the relevant data may be transferred abroad under Article 9 of the KVKK. The transfers rely on the following grounds:

  • Infrastructure services essential to operate the App (Firebase: authentication, database, notifications) — necessity for the performance of the contract and the provision of appropriate safeguards.
  • One-time verification of the mastership certificate (Gemini) — this incidental (one-time) transfer relies on your explicit consent.

7. Retention Periods

  • Mastership certificate image: not stored (processed at the moment of verification, then deleted).
  • Account, listing and HGP data: for as long as your account is active; deleted or anonymized when you delete your account or when statutory retention periods end.

8. Your Rights under the KVKK

Under Article 11 of the KVKK, you have the right to:

  • Learn whether your personal data is processed and, if so, request information about it.
  • Learn the purpose of processing and whether your data is used in accordance with that purpose.
  • Know the third parties to whom your data is transferred, domestically or abroad.
  • Request correction if it is incomplete/inaccurate; request erasure/destruction where the conditions are met, and request that these actions be notified to the third parties to whom the data was transferred.
  • Object to an adverse outcome arising from the analysis of your data solely by automated systems (e.g. rejection of certificate verification — see section 4).
  • Claim compensation if you suffer damage due to unlawful processing.

In addition, for processing that relies on your explicit consent, you may withdraw your consent at any time; withdrawal does not affect processing lawfully carried out before then.

You may submit your requests to ahmetadqzl0244@gmail.com in accordance with the “Communiqué on the Procedures and Principles for Application to the Data Controller”; your request will be concluded within 30 days at the latest.

9. Security

Data is protected against unauthorized access with reasonable technical and administrative measures (hashing of passwords, encryption in transit, HGP balances being modifiable only by the server, API keys kept only on the server, etc.).
